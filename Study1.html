The Challenge

Project Goals

My Approach

  • With the increasing importance of media and technology, there is so much information being broadcasted.
  • Finding information that is factual rather than opinion-based is also difficult.
  • Users want to limit what news and information they see/hear on their interests.
  • There are no local news applications that solve these problems.
  • Create a local-focused news source app to see events and articles.
  • Create an onboarding process that teaches users how to use the app.
  • Allow sign-up and sign-in from third party services.
  • Gather user demographics to personalize the app experience.
  • Allow users to choose what causes they are interested in seeing information for.

  • Let users choose what causes they want to follow to see that information in their personalized feeds.
  • Create a feed that allows users to add events to calendars and bookmark articles.
  • Easily view local government elected officials of user’s area based on zip code.
  • Share articles/events to social media or with friends.

Understanding Competitors

The team completed a competitive analysis and found that there is currently no mobile app dedicated to receiving customized and factual local news. Existing apps are either focused on national news, or show irrelevant, opinion-based information for communities. theSkimm and Apple News send notifications about news in almost real-time but is not customizable, or locally focused. Nextdoor is a local app that allows anyone to post based on your neighborhood, but is more often used to report lost pets, sell items or other non-news postings. Meetup can be used to notify users of gatherings or demonstrations but does not have articles and is mainly used for quick social updates.


Discovering Motivation of Users


We conducted one-on-one interviews with five users to better understand their motivations and frustrations of becoming involved within their communities. Results were synthesized using an affinity map to identify commonalities and organize where in the process obstacles lie.

Local Awareness:

Many users felt that they do not have the resources to learn about local events and news that is of interest to them

Interests:

Users wanted to be able to see information for causes they are interested in. Local education, sustainability, public transportation, election information and more

News Sources:

Most participants revealed that they either use social media or have to look up information in order to find what they are looking for

Information Accessibility:

With the increase in social media, there is much more information being circulated, which users found to be difficult to digest and sort through.

02 Define

Defining the User


After working with users, we developed our user persona, Jamie Mack.
Jamie is an online educator, who is caring, patient, organized yet easily overwhelmed and intimidated by politics.
She is looking to be more involved with her community and wants to stay informed but has a difficult time sorting through the information in order to find factual information.
As someone who values tech and the social aspects of the digital world, an app that is a one stop shop to find local news would be ideal.

Visualizing the User Journey


I wanted to show the user’s experiences without the app and then with the app. Showing the user’s frustrations prior to downloading the app, and the new found capabilities they have after accessing it’s features.

03 Ideation



Exploring Ideas

Our team then created I like, I wish and What If statements and completed dot voting to determine what features would be considered when developing our app. Whether those ideas were as simple as ‘I like that twitter is brief’ or as complex as ‘I wish you could protest through virtual reality’, we were able to think of ideas that could create realistic features.
